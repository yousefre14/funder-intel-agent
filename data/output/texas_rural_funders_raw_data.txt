# Texas Rural Funders is the leading membership organization for philanthropy dedicated to rural Texas and a trusted partner and connector for rural communities.

Why Rural?It’s hard to overestimate the importance of rural Texas to our past, present, and future. Rural land doesn’t just make up 85% of our state-- it’s the heart of our culture, economy, and quality of life. As we like to say, “Your food is not grown in the HEB parking lot.” All Texans depend on rural communities for food, fiber, and fuel. Simply put, a thriving future for Texas is contingent on the well-being of its rural communities.Rural communities, however, face a growing number of challenges that threaten their long-term success. Rapid population growth across Texas, urban sprawl, and ever-increasing demands on land and water resources put constant pressure on rural Texas. High-quality education, economic development, employment, healthcare, and infrastructure—the building-blocks all communities need to thrive—are more difficult to secure in sparsely populated areas. As a result, rural communities trail other parts of the state in measures of long-term well-being.With the rapid urbanization of our state, less information and political attention is currently focused on rural Texas than in past decades. TRF provides funders a unique opportunity and responsibility to raise up and amplify rural voices.

# We mobilize the power of philanthropy to strengthen rural Texas.

About UsTexas Rural Funders is dedicated to helping its members build knowledge about, generate attention and resources toward, and engage in collaborative action for rural Texas. Together, we bring a breadth of experience, resources and influence that none of us could provide alone.We hold a shared belief that thriving rural communities are essential for the success of our state as a whole.Learn More About Joining

## About Us

How We StartedIn 2017, our founding group of funders gathered to consider how—working together—we might mobilize the power of philanthropy to strengthen rural Texas. Our group engaged in deep learning about issues impacting rural parts of the state and began to envision a shared strategy for investment in cross-sector issues impacting rural Texans’ quality of life.We are now an independent nonprofit organization focused on collective action. Texas Rural Funders members are private foundations, community foundations, and a variety of intermediary organizations, both statewide and national, small and large.Our unique interests in rural Texas span healthcare, education, economic development, infrastructure, and natural resources. What unites us all is our interest in increasing attention, resources, and collaborative action that strengthens rural Texas.By leveraging our diverse expertise and co-funding opportunities, we are able to make a bigger, more systemic impact than one funder could alone.
